1. What do you mean by cells in an excel sheet?
ans:	
	In Excel, a cell is the intersection point of a row and a column. Each cell is identified by a unique address, which is a combination of the column letter and the row number. For example, the cell at the intersection of column B and row 6 is referred to as "B6" .

2. How can you restrict someone from copying a cell from your worksheet?
ans:
	To prevent someone from copying a cell in your worksheet following steps should be followed :
	Select the cell or range of cells you want to protect.
	Right-click and choose "Format Cells."
	In the "Protection" tab, uncheck the "Locked" option.
	Go to the "Review" tab, click on "Protect Sheet," and set a PASSWORD if needed.
	Click "OK."
	Now the selected cells data cannot be copied !

3. How to move or copy the worksheet into another workbook?
ans:

	Choose "Move or Copy."
	In the "To book" dropdown, selectthe target workbook. Choose the location withIn the workbook where you want to place the sheet.
	Select whether you want to create a copy or move the sheet.
	Click "OK."
	One worksheet has been copied or moved into another workboook

6. When to use a relative cell reference in excel?
ans:
	For example, if you have a formula in cell B2 as "=A2+C2" and you copy it to cell B3, it will automatically adjust to "=A3+C3."
	Relative references are useful when you want the same calculation relative to the position of the formula in different cells.
